My story

Curiosity first. Then cancer made it personal.

For as long as I can remember I was drawn to how living things work, dissecting bugs in the backyard, watching surgical procedures online, fascinated by what others found uncomfortable. Science was never an obligation. It was just how I saw the world.

When my mother was diagnosed with cancer, that curiosity found its direction. Cancer biology wasn't a field I chose. It chose me. I finished my bachelor's in three years while working multiple jobs, earned my master's conducting research at UCSF, and have now been accepted to Oxford's DPhil program in oncology.

Alongside my research, I've taught over 100 students across subjects from intro biology to human anatomy, founded a student-run anatomy club, and privately tutored biology and chemistry. I believe science is only powerful if it reaches people, and I plan to spend my career making sure it does.

From the scientific method and intro biology to human anatomy with cadaver labs at SFSU. I also privately tutored biology and chemistry. I teach because I know what it feels like to navigate science without a guide.

Founder and President, SOMA Club

I founded the Systems-Oriented Medical Anatomy Club at SFSU to connect anatomy to its clinical meaning through workshops on real surgical and diagnostic applications, open to pre-med, nursing, and biology students.

STEM outreach and peer mentorship

Peer mentor for underrepresented students in STEM. Coordinator of events serving up to 200 participants through SEES outreach. Volunteered with a legal nonprofit supporting individuals leaving high-control groups.
